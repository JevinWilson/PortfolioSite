The Data Set Used

This dataset offers a comprehensive look into global salary trends for roles in Data Science, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, etc.

Column Name Description
work_year The year the salary was reported. Covers salaries from 2020 through 2025.
experience_level The seniority level of the employee at the time of reporting (Entry, Mid, Senior, Exec).
employment_type The type of employment contract (Full-time, Part-time, Contract, Freelance).
job_title The employee’s specific job title (e.g., Data Scientist, ML Engineer, AI Specialist, Research Scientist).
salary The employee's gross annual salary in the original reported currency, before taxes and deductions.
salary_currency The employee's salary converted into USD using 2025 exchange rates for standardized comparison.
employee_residence The country (ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 code) where the employee resides. This may differ from the company location, especially in remote roles.
remote_ratio Indicates the percentage of remote work (0: On-site, 50: Hybrid, 100: Remote).
range_km Estimated driving range on a full charge (km)
company_location The country (ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 code) where the company or employer is headquartered.
company_size The size of the employing organization (S: Small (1–50 employees), M: Medium (51–500 employees), L: Large (501+ employees))
AI/ML Salary Evolution by Experience Level (2020-2025)

How have AI/ML salaries evolved over time and which roles are growing fastest?

AI/ML Salaries Surged 48% But Hit a Ceiling in 2025
Entry-level salaries grew dramatically from $69,841 (2020) to $103,413 (2024) - a 48% increase. However, 2025 shows a 7% decline to $96,141, suggesting market correction after rapid pandemic-era growth. This indicates the AI boom may be stabilizing.
(View "BY EXPERIENCE")

Machine Learning Engineers Command Premium Pay
ML Engineers lead the salary rankings at $201,788 in 2025, earning 95% more than Data Analysts ($103,366). The top-paying roles are ML Engineer, Research Scientist ($198,729), and Software Engineer ($190,518).
(View "BY JOB TITLE")

Traditional Data Roles Show Strongest Growth Rates
While ML roles pay more, traditional data roles showed faster 5-year growth: Data Engineers (+71%), Data Analysts (+70%), and ML Engineers (+68%). Data engineering roles experienced the highest growth, likely due to increased demand for data infrastructure supporting AI initiatives.

AI/ML Salaries by Work Type - All Experience Levels (2024)

Does remote work pay more, less, or the same?

Analysis based on 2024 data, the most recent complete year.

Remote Work Penalty Is Real (But Decreasing)
Contrary to popular belief, remote work typically pays less than on-site roles:

  • Entry Level: 13.2% salary reduction for remote work
  • Mid Level: 12.9% salary reduction
  • Senior Level: 9.2% salary reduction
  • Executive Level: 14.1% salary premium for remote work

Hybrid Work Gets the Worst Deal
Hybrid workers consistently earn the least across all experience levels. Companies appear to view hybrid arrangements as offering neither the collaboration benefits of on-site nor the cost savings of fully remote.

Seniority Flips the Remote Equation
The remote work penalty decreases with experience level and actually becomes a premium at the executive level. This suggests senior skills are more location-independent and executives can command global market rates.

Business Recommendations:

  • Early Career (Entry/Mid): Accept the 10-13% remote discount only if lifestyle benefits (no commute, geographic flexibility) outweigh the financial cost.
  • Senior Level: Remote penalty drops to 9%, making geographic arbitrage viable (earn US salary, live in lower-cost location).
  • Executive Level: Remote work becomes financially advantageous - pursue distributed leadership opportunities.
AI/ML Average Salaries by Company Size (2024)

Do bigger companies really pay more?

Medium Companies Pay Most
Medium-sized companies (51-500 employees) offer the highest executive compensation at $200,923 - that's 52% more than small companies and 20% more than large companies.

Large Companies Underperform Expectations
Large companies (501+ employees) pay executives $160,113 - significantly less than medium companies. This suggests large corporations may rely more on non-cash compensation (stock options, benefits, prestige) rather than base salary to attract talent.

Data Quality and Market Realities
The analysis includes varying sample sizes across company categories, but this actually reflects natural market distribution:

  • Small Companies (8 data points): Very few small companies (1-50 employees) can afford specialized AI/ML roles. The 8 data points likely represent well-funded startups or niche consulting firms.
  • Large Companies (31 data points): In any healthy market, there are naturally fewer large companies. Think about it: How many Googles, Microsofts, or Apples can exist? Market concentration means most large companies are already well-known entities.
  • Medium Companies (4,119 data points): This is the "sweet spot" where companies are big enough to need specialists but small enough to be numerous.

The sample size variation doesn't indicate data quality issues—it reflects the natural distribution of companies in the AI/ML hiring market.
